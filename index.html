üí™ Why Protein Matters in Menopause

During menopause, your body needs more protein to maintain muscle mass, support bone health, stabilise blood sugar, and help you feel fuller for longer.

Lower oestrogen levels accelerate muscle loss, so adequate protein (spread throughout the day) becomes essential for strength, metabolism, and overall wellbeing.

üåü Essential Nutrition Guidelines

Foundation principles for hormonal balance and metabolic health

‚è∞ Meal Timing & Overnight Fasting

  • Eat 3 balanced meals per day - no snacking between meals
  • Last meal at least 1 hour before bed - ideally 2-3 hours for optimal digestion
  • 12-14 hour overnight fast - e.g., finish dinner by 7pm, breakfast at 7-9am
  • Why? Constant eating triggers glucose spikes and insulin resistance. Fasting periods allow insulin levels to drop and support metabolic health.

üö´ Avoid Snacking - Here's Why

  • Snacking causes glucose roller coaster - constant blood sugar spikes and crashes
  • Increases insulin resistance risk - your body never gets a break from processing food
  • Disrupts fat burning - insulin blocks fat metabolism
  • If genuinely hungry between meals: Have a small protein-based snack (boiled egg, handful of nuts, Greek yogurt) - NOT carbs

üèÉ Why Movement Matters in Menopause

Regular physical activity is one of the most effective ways to manage menopause symptoms. Exercise helps regulate hormones, improves mood, supports bone density, aids weight management, and reduces the severity of hot flushes.

The key: Different symptoms respond to different types of movement. This section provides evidence-based protocols for the most common menopause symptoms.

‚ö†Ô∏è Important: Always consult your GP before starting a new exercise programme, especially if you have existing health conditions. Consider working with a qualified physiotherapist, personal trainer, or exercise professional who can assess your individual needs and ensure proper form. Start gradually and listen to your body.
